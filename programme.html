MindLoops® Maker Space Programme Details


The MindLoops® Maker Space programme is an initiative launched by EDESS exclusively along with our partners geared towards primary school children. This annual programme is not just a mere programme, it also provides students with a comprehensive learning experience through the processes involved in the development of the prototypes as planned in this programme.


These experiences focus heavily on STEM and TVET subjects, while also nurturing entrepreneurial thinking skills within students and encouraging skills that are essential in the 21st century. This programme provides schools that are shortlisted by JPNJ with a platform with a platform to build a team of students to participate in the Maker Space competition.


Each team is given the task of choosing a theme and develop a conceptual prototype for a usable product. After the concept has been approved by the judges, the teams shall start develop the product within the given timeframe.


The final event of this programme will be the announcement of the winner among the 22 teams, and the award ceremony in which the team the shows their utmost effort and creativity in the development of their prototypes will be receiving medals (gold, silver, and bronze).



Programme Goals


  • To nurture the development of 21st century skills through activities that focus on STEM, TVET and SDG.

  • To encourage the culture of innovation, entrepreneurship, and mastery of new technologies.

  • To encourage sustainable practices that can benefit the future.


Programme Mission


To build a dynamic environment that encourages the development 21st century skills through practical implementations of STEM, TVET, and SDG, nurturing entrepreneurial thinking, and global citizenship values.



Programme Objectives


  • To develop thinking skills that are creative, entrepreneur-minded, and innovative through activities that use imagination in order to come up with solutions in problems related to STEM, TVET, and SDG.

  • To promote teamwork and effective communication among teammates in order to develop leadership qualities within participants.

  • To educate participants on digital literacy and sustainable problem solving in order to handle global challenges ethically.


Target Participants


This state-level programme targets primary schools students across Johor. Below are the details of the participant


  1. Approx. total of participants: 88 students (ratio of students to teachers 4:1)
  2. Age range: 10 to 12 years old
  3. Gender: Male and Female
  4. School type: Government, private, and international
  5. Location: Across Johor


Participant Details


  • During the idea conception stage, participation is open to all primary schools in the state of Johor that are interested in participating.

  • Registration is limited to 22 teams as the finalists for the prototype development and presentation stage.

  • Each team is comprised of 4 participants and are led by an (1) accompanying teacher.


Registration Criteria


Each team is instructed to develop a model based on the criterias below


  • Innovation: The originality of the product developed

  • Materials: Participants are instructed to use recycled materials such as paper, cardboard, glass, metals and plastic to build a model

  • Functionalities: The product developed must function according to the theme

  • Safety: The product must be safe to use

  • Design: The product must be suitable and interesting


Programme Stages


There are three (3) stages that participants will go thorugh, which are:


  • Stage 1: Idea Conception

    • This stage will be ongoing for 6 weeks before the day on which the programme begins.
    • Open to all schools in the state of Johor interested in registering.
    • Accompanying teachers must attend a briefing session by the programme organisers.
    • Each team must provide a description of the prototype idea that they plan on developing on the form provided.
    • Students must study and conduct research on a suitable idea. The original idea must be made up-to-date using artificial intelligence technologies (AI) under guidance. Examples of AI applications include ChatGPT, CoPilot, Perplexity, etc.
    • Each team must choose the type of prototypes, functionalities, and materials that are suitable with their themes and target user demographics.
    • In this stage, each team will discuss the idea along with conforming the prototypes they intend on development.
    • Each team is REQUIRED to use recycled materials, and tools that suitable.
    • Each team must fill in a Maker Space Project Planning form to be assessed and filtered.
    • Only 22 teams will be selected to continue to the final stage after the elimination process.

  • Stage 2: Development Stage

    • This stage occurs on the day of the programme.
    • Only attended by the 22 finalist teams that have passed the preliminary stage.
    • Only 2 hours are given to develop the prototype.
    • Each team must bring their own materials and tools. (The organisers will not be providing recycled materials. Schools must provide any materials required as per their prototype plan). Essential tools and materials such as glue, scissors, and tape are provided by the organiser.
    • Task division is subjective. It may be subject to change depending on the situation or requirements. Therefore, students must be focused and apply their teamwork when completing their tasks.
    • A representative from each group will be called upon by the judges to present the Entrepreneurial Concept for their prototype based on the Business Canvas for 5 minutes. This presentation will be held during the building process
    • Each team must check and test their prototypes before presenting in front of the judges and audience.

  • Stage 3: Presentation

    • This stage occurs on the day of the programme.
    • Each team must have a representative to present their prototype in front of the competition's judges.
    • The time given for each presentation is 3 minutes (3-Minute Pitch)
    • The presenting representative must have great, clear and effective communication skills
